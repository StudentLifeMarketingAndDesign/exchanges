Georgi Borissov’s poetry is very musical in its native Bulgarian. Like most of Borissov’s poems, “Jonah” makes use of rhythm and rhyme in a quatrain form. It was a challenge to translate, knowing that all three elements could not be directly transferred from the original language into English. I considered the form to be immutable, and I chose to honor the rhythm as much as possible. I was not able to accommodate the end rhymes of the original without significant sacrifice to the artistic quality of the work. Therefore, wherever I could, I incorporated internal rhyme to help preserve the musical quality of the poem.
