Midsummer Night

Katherine Factor

Come, the boy said and took the girl’s hand.
Firesparks whirled and gleamed in the girl’s eyes.
Will he take me to dance, thought the girl, will he at last?
Come, said the boy, away into the woods. Just awhile.
The fire was flaring, the shadows dancing, two
dipped into darkness.
Will he hold me, thought the girl, will he hold me at last?
The nightbird’s cry pulsed through both.
The fern flower! I’ve found it, said the boy. Look!
In the darkness a lonely light sparkled.
If only he’d kiss me, thought the girl, kiss me at last.
Big deal, I’ve seen the glow-worm before.
No more words. So. Silently they went back,
The fire flowered as it wills, the accordion played a half waltz.
Suppose I should go now, the boy said in a halved voice.
The girl gulped. Yes, I guess that you will.
