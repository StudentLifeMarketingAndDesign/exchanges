Rosario Sanmiguel was born in the northern state of Chihuahua, Mexico. She is well known for her acclaimed collection of short stories, Callejón Sucre y otros relatos. The Mexican writer, Eduardo Antonio Parra, has written that "life along the border has found its greatest literary depiction in Callejón Sucre." In 2006, Sanmiguel published her second book of fiction, a short novel entitled, Árboles o apuntes de viaje. In the years between the publication of the two books, Sanmiguel dedicated her time to editing journals and books of poetry as well as to literary translation. In addition, she works as a professor at the Autonomous University of Ciudad Juárez, teaching classes on Twentieth-Century Mexican and Latin American Literature. Currently, she is at work on her second novel.

John Pluecker is an interpreter, translator, and writer in Houston, Texas. His literary work has been published in Clamor Magazine, the Julie Mango Journal, Altanoche and New Texas, among others. He recently published a scholarly introduction to Conrado Espinosa's 1926 novel of Mexican immigration, El sol de Texas (Under the Texas Sun). In 2008, Arte Público Press will publish his book-length translation of the short stories of Rosario Sanmiguel entitled Bajo el Puente: Relatos desde la Frontera (Under the Bridge: Stories from the Border). He has also translated a number of children's books for Arte Público Press. He maintains a blog with his writings at badtexas.blogspot.com.
