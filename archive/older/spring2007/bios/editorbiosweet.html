Becka Mara McKay (left) is fairly certain she has been in graduate school for more than 100 years. She earned an MFA in translation from the University of Iowa and an MFA in creative writing from the University of Washington, and is currently working on her PhD in comparative literature. She tries to leave Iowa whenever possible, and as a result has visited Morocco, Bangladesh, and Israel in the last year. She translates poetry and fiction from modern Hebrew, and her work can be found in ACM, American Letters & Commentary, Columbia, Controlled Burn, Cranky, Hayden’s Ferry Review, Third Coast, and TWO LINES. In 2004 she received a fellowship from the American Literary Translators Association.

Emily Weirich (right) has had a dictionary somewhere on her person since she bought a copy of Fleurs de Mal (yes, she was 14) at a second-hand bookshop in her hometown of Lansing, Michigan. Since then, the added weight hasn’t been able to hold her down, and she’s made her home in Amboise (France), Ann Arbor, Beijing, Shanghai, New York, and now Iowa City. She’s currently translating the poetry of the 12th-century Chinese poet Zhu Shuzhen, for which she needs many, many, many dictionaries.
