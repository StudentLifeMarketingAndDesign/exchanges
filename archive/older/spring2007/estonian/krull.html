Two poems

(1)

Balti jaamas. Varane keskpäev, hiline hommik,
rohelise turu pika tühja rohelise leti ääres
päike paistab otse silma, lumi sulab
silmaga nähtavalt, mööda lähevad igapäevased rändurid
ja Balti jaama alalised elanikud.

Kui lume sisse visata koni, siis lumi ei lähe mustaks, lumi
on ikka valge ja pimestab ja sulab. Suur tõsine vanamees
seisab keset teed, lähen temast kaarega ringi, ta hingab
sügavalt sisse ja teeb kepiga esimese sammu.

Rongis
nii aeglaselt
pöörab päike
raudteele vastu, müts ja kindad
vastasistmel
väsinud ja
soojad.

 
Two poems

(1)

In the Baltic station. Early midday, late morning,
next to the empty green counter of the green market,
the sun shines straight into your eye, the snow melts
visibly. The everyday wanderers and the permanent
inhabitants of the Baltic station go past.

If you throw a cigarette stub into the snow, the snow
doesn’t get dirty. It will still be white and will still
dazzle. It will still melt. The big, serious old man
stands in the middle of the road. I go past, giving him
a wide berth, he breathes in deeply and makes the first step
with his stick.

On the train
so slowly
the sun turns
against the railroad, the hat and gloves
on the opposite bench
tired
and warm.

 

Hasso Krull (trans. Brandon Lussier & Eva Liina Asu-Garcia)
